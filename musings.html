Twice the speed of light

The speed of light is as fast as things go, right?

A simple thought experiment indicates otherwise. It seems that the maximum speed in the universe is maybe twice the speed of light

I am sharing this musing here because I have never heard a clear explanation as to why it is wrong. Maybe you can help?

2c or not 2c?

Imagine you are at a train station and a train whips by at 30 m/s. At the same time another train is travelling in the opposite direction at the same speed. After one second either train is 30 m from you but 60 m from each other. In other words, relative to you they both have a speed of 30 m/s but relative to each other it is 60 m/s.

More generally, if one train have a velocity of x m/s and the other -x m/s then they have a relative speed of 2x m/s.

Conclusion

Two photons travelling in opposite directions have a relative speed of 2c. Since all speed is relative the maximum speed in the universe is 2c.
